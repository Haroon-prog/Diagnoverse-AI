HbA1c measures average blood sugar over 3 months. Values above 6.5% indicate diabetes.

Triglycerides above 500 mg/dL are considered very high and increase risk of heart disease.

Cholesterol is a fatty substance in blood. High levels increase risk of heart disease.

HDL cholesterol is good cholesterol. Low levels increase risk.

LDL cholesterol is bad cholesterol. High levels increase risk.

Creatinine is a kidney function marker. High levels may indicate kidney problems.

Metformin is used to control blood sugar in diabetes.

Amlodipine is used to treat high blood pressure.
